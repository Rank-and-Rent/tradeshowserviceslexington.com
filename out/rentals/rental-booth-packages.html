Rental Booth Packages

Rental booth packages in Lexington bundle structure, graphics, lighting, and freight into a single price, which simplifies the buy for programs that don't need a custom design phase.

Package rentals trade design flexibility for budget clarity. The package has a fixed look and a fixed price; the program adds graphics and shows up.

Lexington package rentals work for first-time exhibitors at Central Bank Center, regional programs at the downtown hotels, and recruiting events at UK or EKU where the buy needs to be simple.

Operationally, packages install fast because the configuration is pre-set. Labor calls and show-services orders are predictable rather than custom-quoted.

These pages cover what package rentals include, how the fixed-price bundle compares to a configured rental, and when the package path is the right call.

  • Keep Rental Booth Packages tied to the actual show outcome, not a vague category label.

  • Leave room for storage, reuse, and return freight so the next cycle starts cleanly.

How Lexington floor operations work

Floor operations for rental booth packages follow the venue's posted rules. Central Bank Center publishes a current rules and dimensions document; downtown hotels distribute their own standards through the property events team.

Utility orders run through each venue's portal or order form, with discount cutoffs at 30 to 45 days before show open. Late orders cost more and may miss the install window.

Rigging approvals at Central Bank Center go through the venue's posted path; hotel ballrooms generally don't support rigging at the convention-center scale.

Labor calls coordinate with each venue's house team. Outside crews need EAC paperwork filed with the show GC or property before they can badge in.

The non-downtown event districts are Newtown Pike to the north (Griffin Gate), south Lexington's hotel belt (Campbell House, Embassy Suites Lexington Green), and the regional Bluegrass corridor (Versailles, Georgetown, Richmond, Frankfort, Nicholasville, Winchester).

Line items on a typical project

Predictable line items show up on most rental booth packages projects; the proportions change with the venue, the build, and the calendar.

Typical line items include booth structure (custom, modular, or rental), graphics and print, AV and lighting, show-services orders (power, internet, rigging) through the venue, labor calls against the venue's rules, freight in and out, on-site supervision, and post-show closeout.

Handoff discipline is the silent variable. Programs with fewer vendors and tighter handoffs land cleaner than programs with more layers.

Multi-show programs add a maintenance layer: asset storage, graphic rotation, refurb scheduling, and re-crating around the planned freight loop.

Out of scope by default are show organizer fees, booth lease costs paid to the show, client staff travel and per diems, and any data or lead-capture software the client runs internally.

How a project runs

Project kickoff for rental booth packages is a brief, not a deck. Show, venue, booth number, calendar, budget all pin down in the first call.

Venue coordination runs second. The exhibitor manual gets walked, dock times confirm, rigging files, labor calls go in writing.

Production runs against locked venue details: fabrication, graphics, AV, rentals, and the on-site playbook all draft together.

Floor leadership stays with one person. They carry the plan, the labor calls, and the venue conversations from crate-open to doors.

The show-day pattern is the same lead, still on the floor, handling tech and venue moves while client staff works visitors.

Closeout: strike on schedule, debrief inside 24 hours, document driving the next planning cycle.

Pricing and scheduling

Pricing for rental booth packages scales with the venue's published rates, the booth's footprint, the freight pattern, and the labor calls. There's no single number that fits every program.

The variables that matter: structure (custom, modular, or rental), graphics, venue service fees, freight, supervision days, and any rigging or AV that pushes outside standard line items. Two rental booth packages programs with different footprints are different projects.

Custom builds for Lexington shows run best with 16 to 20 weeks of lead time. Rentals run 8 to 12. Anything inside 4 weeks is a rush, which means fewer options and a premium on materials.

How project performance gets measured

The numbers on a rental booth packages program live in two places: the floor execution scorecard, and the lead and pipeline ledger that runs after strike.

Both layers have to land. Clean execution with no pipeline is wasted spend; pipeline with venue callouts is reputation damage.

Closing the loop happens in the debrief. The booth's specific zones get scored against attendee behavior and that data drives how the next rental booth packages cycle plans.

Rental Booth Packages planning questions

Do you work at Hyatt Regency Lexington?

Yes. the downtown Hyatt is part of the convention-hotel ring tied directly to the Central Bank Center campus. Projects at that building are planned against its specific floor rules.

How does labor work in Lexington?

It runs venue by venue. Central Bank Center: the dimensions and specification sheet makes the convention campus a real exhibit and conference environment with current floor and access considerations that should shape the brief early. Other Lexington venues have their own published rules, and the plan matches the specific building.

How much lead time is ideal?

Custom builds run best with 16 to 20 weeks of lead time. Rentals take 8 to 12 weeks. Work inside 4 weeks is possible but raises costs and narrows the options.

What information kicks off a project?

The show, the venue, the booth number, the dates, and a target budget range. From those, an initial scope and a realistic cost come back within a few days.

Who runs the booth on-site?

One named show-site lead runs the install, the show hours, and the strike. That person carries the floor plan and the punch list from crates to doors and reports up once a day.

Can the booth travel to the next city on our calendar?

Yes — builds are designed for modular reuse unless single-use scenic is specifically required. Same structure, swappable graphics, standard crates. Lexington programs that roll to another city on the same calendar get handled end to end.

What happens if something breaks during show hours?

The on-site lead handles it. Minor repairs happen in place; anything larger gets sourced from the local shop or rerouted through the venue's service desk.
